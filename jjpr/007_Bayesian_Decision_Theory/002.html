================================================================================ Since Gaussian probability distribution is very much used, Gaussian probability distribution is also called Normal distribution ================================================================================ The reason that Gaussian distribution is much used: - When you use 1D feature vector, it is enough to find normal distribution with 2 paramters; mean $$$\mu$$$ and std $$$\sigma$$$ - Due to central limit theorem, there are many cases where you need to use Gaussian distribution ================================================================================ central limit theorem - Suppose mean $$$\mu$$$, variance $$$\sigma^2$$$ of population. - Since it's difficult to analyze entire population, you extract sample from population. - As size of sample becomes bigger and bigger, that sample becomes data which has $$$\mu$$$ and $$$\frac{\sigma^2}{N}$$$ ================================================================================ Example. - Suppose you want to predict average height of entire population. - You would extract sample. - Suppose you extract 1 person and you calculate average height of that person Then, you will get this distribution - Suppose you extract 4 people and you calculate average height of that people Then, you will get this distribution - Suppose you extract 7 people and you calculate average height of that people Then, you will get this distribution - Suppose you extract 10 people and you calculate average height of that people Then, you will get this distribution - As you increase size of sample, distribution becomes Gaussian distribution more and more. ================================================================================ Multivariate Gaussian probability distribution function $$$f_X(X)= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}^{\frac{n}{2}} |\Sigma|^{\frac{1}{2}}} \exp \left[ -\frac{1}{2} (X-\mu)^T \Sigma^-1 (X-\mu) \right]$$$ Shape of Gaussian distribution is determined by $$$\Sigma$$$ $$$\mu$$$ only affects the peak, not for the shape. ================================================================================ If data has following distribution that data has following covariance $$$\Sigma = \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^2&&c_{12}\\c_{12}&&\sigma_2^2 \end{bmatrix}$$$ ================================================================================ If data has following distribution that data has following covariance $$$\Sigma = \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^2&&0\\0&&\sigma_2^2 \end{bmatrix}$$$ $$$0$$$: correlation of data has none $$$\sigma_1^2 > \sigma_2^2$$$ ================================================================================ If data has following distribution that data has following covariance $$$\Sigma = \begin{bmatrix} \sigma^2&&0\\0&&\sigma^2 \end{bmatrix}$$$ $$$0$$$: correlation of data has none $$$\sigma^2 = \sigma^2$$$ ================================================================================ It also means that you can know the distribution of data by looking at the shape of covariance matrix ================================================================================
